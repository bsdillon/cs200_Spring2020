Homework 14 - Semester Review Questions

  1. Some research shows that JavaScript is a difficult language for novice programmers to learn. This class was taught with JavaScript because the applications built in HTML/CSS/JavaScript are transferable anywhere. You can create a website or blog to showcase your work. You can also write tools to use on your computer as a web page. That web page can be shared with your instructor and with others. Your project game, for example, can be sent to your brother, mother, grandpa, and friends. Answer the following questions:
    • Compared with the syntax of C, C++, Python, Java, Cow, etc. how do you feel about the syntax for JavaScript? Would you recommend a different language for future classes?
    • What problems did you encounter in using JavaScript? What was the most frustrating?
    • Were you happy with the easy portability of the code? Would you have preferred to use a different language which could make apps for your phone? Is there another use case you would have preferred?
  2. This class was set up to meet several objectives. You are getting an overview of a lucrative and valuable skillset. You are earning transferable college credits. You are getting exposure to the kind of work at Dahlgren and the student programs for CS students. Answer the following questions:
    • Review the program information for SSEP program. If you are a dual enrollment student or if you at the top of your game, you might consider the NREIP and SMART programs. Which program would best serve your future career plans?
    • Based on this class, are you more or less interested in a career in programming? Why?
    • What did you learn from the class? What would you have changed? What would you definitely keep for next time?
    • What could I have done to make the class better for you?
  3. Read "Every Major's Terrible". What are your current career plans? Are they in this song?
  4. I sometimes hear non-programmers refer to this kind of work as boring. Among developers, when you finally get it to work or when the program works as you expected the first time, the rush is so good that they tend to literally dance in the halls. Alternatively, programming has been described as spending your whole life searching for a missing '}'. Did you experience one or both? Describe your experience.
  5. Based on the "Every Major's Terrible" song and your experience in this class, how have your career plans altered? Are you excited about your intended major?
