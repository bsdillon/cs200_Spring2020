  2. Watch the first two minutes of this video. The speaker talks about the same bandwidth issue discussed in XKCD. She explains that in Big O notation “constant beats linear if data is sufficiently big”. Let’s prove that. Do/answer the following:
    • Suppose that you have a network speed that allows you to transfer 1 Megabyte (10^6) per second. How long will it take to transfer 1 TeraByte (10^12) of data? If the pigeon takes 24 hours to transfer a 1 TeraByte hard drive, who wins? Calculate the Bytes/minute for each method and compare.
    • Let’s suppose the network speed increases to 1 Gigabyte (10^9) per second. Recalculate the Bytes/minute. Who wins now?
    • Finally if you want to transfer a petabyte (10^15) in the same system (one hard drive by pigeon or by network), who wins now? Recalculate the Bytes/min.
    • What does this tell you about the race between the network and the pigeon? If the pigeon is losing, what can you do to change the answer?
  3. You are mowing each of the lawns below. Your mower is one square long and wide. You are inefficient if you have to pass over a square that you already mowed. Draw your most efficient path. Count the number of revisited squares for each lawn.

  5. Suppose that you are looking for the one silver coin dated 1964 in a bag of n coins. Bob is looking through another identical bag. You open your bag and look at every single coin, finding the one 1964 coin last in the bag. Bob opens his bag, but he finds the coins the first time he dips his hand into the bag. Bob says you were doing it the slow way. Is he right? Why?
  6. You are looking through a bag of 1000 coins. Bob is looking through a bag of 10 coins. You both find the coin last in the bag. He argues that you are much slower than him. Is he right? Why?
  7. Every day you and Bob do the same job on hundreds of bags. Sometimes you find the coin pretty early, sometimes not. On average you find the coin when you are half way through the bag. In Big O that would be O(½n). Bob argues that it’s really just O(n) because we don’t care about the ½ coefficient. Is he right? Why?
  8. One day you go through the whole bag and don’t find a single coin with the right date. It ends up not being there. Your search was O(n) when your average is O(½n). Are you slower? Why?
