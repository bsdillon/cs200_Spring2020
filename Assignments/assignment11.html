Assignment #11 - Person security audit

Purpose
Instructions
Work with another student on this project. Exchange social media accounts, share phones, etc. as necessary to answer the following questions about the other person's online activity and personal security practices. Take away one point for answering yes to a red question. Add one point for answering yes to a blue question.
  1. Do you have a phone? If so:
    • Do you have a password to protect it?
    • Is it a good password (i.e. longer than 4 digits, not your birthday, not 'password', etc.)?
    • Can you open your phone with your fingerprint or face?
    • Open settings and look for Apps > Permissions (Android), Privacy (iPhone), or whatever weird thing you have. Check whether each app needs the permissions it needs (e.g. Food Lion does not need access to my camera). Lose a point for each app that doesn't need a permission.
    • Open settings and look for General > Storage (iPhone). If you are a weirdo or on Android try typing *#*#4636#*#* into your phone. This will open the testing activity. Select Usage statistics > Useage time > Last time used. Lose a point for each app that hasn't been used in more than 90 days.
    • Is your Location Reporting turned on?
    • Is your location embedded in your photos?
  2. For each social media account (twitter, FaceBook, etc) lose a point if you have given them:
    • Real address.
    • Real birthday
    • Real phone number
    • Picture of your home
  3. For each social media account, do you have a good password?
  4. For each of these lose a point if you have ever done the following with someone you have never met in real life:
    • Given your email/phone
    • Told them where you go to school
    • Sent a picture of yourself (regardless of clothing).
    • Told them you were going out of town.
    • Talked about the work you/your parents do.
    • Talked about sex/bodies AT ALL.
  5. If you have a personal computer or laptop
    • Do you have a password to protect it?
    • Is it a good password (i.e. longer than 4 digits, not your birthday, not 'password', etc.)?
    • Do you have a firewall?
    • Is the firewall turned on?
    • Do you have virus protection?
    • Is virus protection turned on?
  6. For each of these lose a point if you have ever done the following with your computer/phone:
    • Looked at pornography literally anywhere.
    • Watched a movie (other than an a paid for services like YouTube, Amazon Prime or Netflix).
    • Listened to music (again other than paid for services).
    • Played a game (again other than paid for services like ArmorGames).
    • Downloaded movies/music/games to your computer.
    • Downloaded software from a company you do not know (i.e. if you get it from Google or Apple, it's fine).
  7. BONUS ROUND: Give your phone to a parent (I don't care if you are an adult or not). Lose a point for every photo on your phone that they think is inappropriate, no matter if you took it or if someone gave it to you.
  8. BONUS ROUND: Lose 100 points if you lied about the last step or deleted anything before giving it to your parent.
  9. BONUS ROUND: Gain 10 points each if can find any of the following about your instructor:
    • A picture of him or his family
    • His address
    • His birthday
    • His email (no fair finding it on the syllabus!)
    • His phone number (no fair finding it on the syllabus!)
    • Where he went to school (high school through college)
  10. Write about this experience in 2-3 paragraphs, including your final score. Do you feel personally secure in your use of technology?
  11. Put all of this on a single web page and submit the link to the page in Canvas.
  12. Post your link and your score in the discussion space on Canvas.
